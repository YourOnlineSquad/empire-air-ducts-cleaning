The Reality of Commercial Grease Fires

Every day your commercial kitchen operates, it vaporizes pounds of cooking oils, animal fats, and grease. Your hood exhaust system is designed to pull this hazardous, smoke-filled air out of the kitchen. However, as this hot grease vapor travels up the metal ductwork toward the roof, it cools, condenses, and forms a highly flammable, resin-like coating on every surface it touches.

For high-volume restaurants in Murrieta running deep fryers, wok stations, or charbroilers, this grease accumulates at an astonishing rate. If an unexpected flare-up occurs on the cooktop, the flames can easily reach the baffle filters. If those filters and the ductwork behind them are coated in accumulated grease, the fire will instantly travel up the exhaust shaft. This transforms your ventilation system into a blast furnace, rapidly spreading the fire to the roof and threatening the entire structure.

Routine restaurant hood deep cleaning is not merely a cosmetic chore to keep the stainless steel looking shiny. It is the absolute most critical fire prevention measure your restaurant must undertake to protect your staff, your patrons, and your business investment from total loss.

Chemical Emulsification We apply industrial-strength, caustic degreasers (like sodium hydroxide foams) to the baffle filters, the canopy interior, the vertical ductwork, and the rooftop upblast fan.
