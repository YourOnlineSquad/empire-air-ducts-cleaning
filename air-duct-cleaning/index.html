NADCA Guidelines

The Science of Source Removal Extraction

At Empire Air Ducts, we adhere strictly to the National Air Duct Cleaners Association standard known as Source Removal. This is the only scientifically proven method to safely and permanently remove contaminants from an HVAC system without pushing them back into your breathable airspace.

Step 1 System Isolation and Negative Pressure
We begin by sealing off your supply and return registers. We then attach a massive commercial grade vacuum unit equipped with hospital grade HEPA filters directly to your main HVAC trunk line. This places the entire system under intense negative pressure, creating a powerful one way airflow out of your house.

Step 2 Pneumatic Agitation
While the system is under this intense suction, we introduce specialized pneumatic tools, such as compressed air whips and rotary brushes, deep into the ductwork. These tools safely but aggressively scrub the interior walls of your flex and rigid metal ducts.

Step 3 Safe Eradication
As decades of caked on dust, dead skin cells, construction debris, and pet dander are dislodged by the whips, the negative pressure instantly pulls the hazardous particulate matter out of your home and directly into our contained filtration system.

Indoor Air Quality

The Hidden Impact of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Your ductwork acts as the lungs of your property. In Southern California, factors like the Santa Ana winds, prolonged dry heat, and seasonal wildfires create an environment where fine particulate matter constantly infiltrates your home.

The Health Toll
When microscopic allergens, volatile organic compounds, and mold spores settle in your dark temperature controlled ducts, they multiply. Every time your blower motor turns on, these irritants are aerosolized, triggering asthma attacks, unseasonal allergies, and chronic respiratory fatigue in your family or employees.

The Financial Toll
The danger is not just to your lungs it is to your wallet. Even a thin millimeter of dust coating your evaporator coil and blower motor forces your HVAC unit to work up to 30 percent harder to maintain climate control. By clearing these blockages, professional extraction restores critical airflow, lowers your monthly cooling costs, and prevents catastrophic multi thousand dollar HVAC motor failures.

Knowledge Base

Air Duct Extraction FAQ

In depth technical answers to help you make an informed decision regarding your property ventilation health.

Will the negative air pressure vacuum damage my flexible ductwork?

No. Our advanced extraction equipment is meticulously calibrated to safely handle all types of residential and commercial ventilation. Whether you have modern flexible ducting, traditional rigid sheet metal, or older fiberglass duct board, our pneumatic whips are designed to be gentle on the duct walls while remaining highly effective at dislodging built up debris.

Why shouldn't I hire a budget carpet cleaner to clean my air ducts?

Many budget blow and go companies utilize standard shop vacuums that lack the raw CFMs required to place an entire home HVAC system under true negative pressure. Without this pressure and commercial HEPA filtration, these budget services often just agitate the dust and blow it directly out of your vents and into your living space, making your indoor air quality significantly worse than before.

What is HVAC sanitization, and do I actually need it?

HVAC sanitization is an optional add on that involves fogging an EPA registered botanical antimicrobial solution throughout your freshly cleaned ductwork. We highly recommend this service if your system has previously suffered from roof leaks or moisture issues, has a lingering musty odor, or if you are moving into a previously occupied home and want to permanently neutralize existing biological contaminants left by previous owners or pets.

How long does a comprehensive extraction take?

Because we do not cut corners, a standard extraction for a single family home approximately 1,500 to 2,500 square feet with one central HVAC system typically takes between 2.5 to 4 hours. Larger commercial properties or multi story homes with dual HVAC units will naturally require additional time to ensure every branch line is fully cleared.
